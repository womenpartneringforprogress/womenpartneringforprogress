There are many barriers to leadership for women in Indiana

  • Women are underrepresented and underdeveloped in leadership roles

    In fact, Indiana was given a C+ grade overall for women in management positions and elected office. The gap is even greater for women of color.

  • Civic leadership is not accessible to women in Indiana

    Despite contributing 58% of the volunteer hours nationwide, women hold less than XX% of the positions on non-profit governing boards.

  • Women are left out of critical public policy discussions

    Indiana was given an F grade and ranked 45th of all states for the well-being of women based on 36 factors related to women's economic security, leadership, and health.

Our Organization

We created Women Partnering for Progress in response

We are creating progressive change in Hoosier communities by connecting high potential women leaders. We believe that a progressive future in Indiana must be powered by strong, diverse, and effective women leaders who have the skills and knowledge ready to build solutions as great as the challenges we face.

WPP trains and connects motivated women who unabashedly support one another in positively impacting the areas where they work and live. Our network of leaders drives collaboration across sectors in order to improve the quality of our institutions and amplify the voices of women in policy making.
